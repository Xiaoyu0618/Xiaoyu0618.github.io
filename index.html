Biography

Xiaoyu Li is a senior undergraduate majoring in Soil and Water Conservation and Desertification Combating at Beijing Forestry University. My research experience includes developing ultra-high-resolution land use datasets from drone imagery using machine learning algorithms, as well as monitoring and predicting vegetation dynamics and ecosystem health through multiple remote sensing sources and modeling approaches. I have actively participated in five national research projects, including the Second Tibetan Plateau Scientific Expedition and Research Program. To date, I have published three first-author papers and released two open-access datasets. Two additional manuscripts are currently under review. These experiences have provided me with a strong foundation in remote sensing, ecology, and UAV. I have also developed strong skills in data collection, analysis, and visualization, with proficiency in ArcGIS Pro, GEE, Python, R and so on. Beyond academic research, I have co-authored a book, contributed to a scientific atlas, published a popular science article, and created a science outreach video showcasing aerial imagery of the Tibetan Plateau.

Rural human living environment

Using satellite and UAV remote sensing data combined with objective statistical datasets, we constructed a multi-dimensional assessment framework for evaluating rural human settlement environments in the Tibetan Plateau. Validated by villager satisfaction surveys, this study demonstrates the feasibility and advantages of multi-source data approaches, providing a scientific foundation for settlement improvement and revitalization projects in high-altitude areas.

Village-scale land use composition

We employed object-based segmentation and machine learning algorithms to classify ultra-high-resolution UAV imagery collected from villages. This study provides unprecedented large-sample, high-accuracy land use data for villages in high-altitude regions. We analyzed the land use composition, influencing factors, and spatial patterns of these villages, offering valuable insights for land-use optimization and rural revitalization strategies. Additionally, based on this dataset, we assessed the extent and spatial distribution of village green spaces in 2022. Utilizing the GEE platform, we analyzed temporal trends in green land from the past 23 years and identified potential areas for improvement using multivariate regression models incorporating natural and socioeconomic factors. This represents the first comprehensive, village-scale green land study on the Tibetan Plateau, providing guidance for planning.

Ecosystem health

We used the Vigor-Organization-Resilience (VOR) model to assess ecosystem health changes on the Tibetan Plateau from 1980 to 2020. Based on CA-Markov model predictions of 2030 vegetation cover and land use, we forecast future ecosystem health trends. The results offer scientific support for ecological monitoring, land-use planning, and policy-making in the region.

Vegetation dynamics

Using a comprehensive set of influencing factors, combined with Mann-Kendall trend tests and Geographical Detector analysis, we examined spatiotemporal changes in vegetation over the past 20 years and identified key driving factors. This approach addresses previous limitations in using single methods or individual factors when examining vegetation NDVI in high-altitude human settlements, providing scientific evidence to support ecological improvement efforts in northeastern Tibetan Plateau.
